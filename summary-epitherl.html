I. General characteristics of epithelial tissue

Definition • polarity • avascularity
  • Closely packed polyhedral cells with minimal extracellular matrix.
  • Cells form continuous sheets covering surfaces or lining cavities.
  • Polarity: apical, lateral, basal domains are distinct.
  • Avascular: no blood vessels inside epithelium.
  • Rest on a basement membrane.
  • High regenerative capacity.
MU-VARNA TRAP
  • Blood vessels do not penetrate epithelium.
  • Nerve fibers may penetrate epithelium.

II. Basement membrane

Basal lamina • reticular lamina

Structure

  • Basal lamina: secreted by epithelial cells.
  • Contains type IV collagen, laminin, nidogen, perlecan.
  • Reticular lamina: connective tissue origin (type III collagen).
  • Connected by type VII collagen anchoring fibrils.

Functions

  • Mechanical support.
  • Selective filtration.
  • Maintains epithelial polarity.
  • Scaffold for regeneration.
EXAM ALERT
  • Basement membrane ≠ basal lamina (basal lamina is only part).

III. Intercellular junctions

Tight • adherens • desmosomes • gap junctions
Junction Main proteins Cytoskeleton Function
Tight junction Claudin, occludin — Seal, polarity
Adherens junction Cadherins Actin Mechanical linkage
Desmosome Desmoglein Intermediate filaments (keratin) Strong adhesion
Gap junction Connexins — Cell communication
CLASSIC TRAP
  • Tight junctions block paracellular transport, not transcellular.
  • Desmosomes are linked to intermediate filaments, not actin.

IV. Apical specializations

Microvilli • stereocilia • cilia
Structure Cytoskeleton Function Location
Microvilli Actin Increase surface area Intestine
Stereocilia Actin Absorption / sensation Epididymis, inner ear
Cilia Microtubules (9+2) Movement of fluid Respiratory tract
MU-VARNA TRAP
  • Stereocilia are not true cilia.
  • Cilia contain microtubules, not actin.

V. Types of epithelia

Classification rules
Epithelium Location Main function
Simple squamous Endothelium, mesothelium Diffusion
Simple cuboidal Thyroid, ovary Secretion
Simple columnar Intestine Absorption
Pseudostratified ciliated Trachea Mucociliary clearance
Stratified squamous keratinized Epidermis Protection
Transitional epithelium Urinary bladder Distension
EXCEPT PATTERN
  • Classification is based on surface cells only.
  • Pseudostratified epithelium is simple, not stratified.

VI. Renewal & transport

Stem cells • transcellular vs paracellular
  • Epithelial renewal via stem cells in basal layer.
  • Transport across epithelium is:
    • Transcellular (through cells).
    • Paracellular (between cells, controlled by tight junctions).
Clinical anchor

Loss of ciliary function → chronic respiratory infections (Kartagener syndrome).
